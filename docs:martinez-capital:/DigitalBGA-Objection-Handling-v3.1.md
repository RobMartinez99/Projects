# Opening Objections

Practice brevity. Keep responses short, simple and confident.

The more you dwell on an objection, the bigger it becomes for the prospect.

## Free Insurance

## “Is this free?”

“No, the coverage itself isn’t free. These are state-regulated final expense plans designed so everyone on a limited and fixed income can afford them.”

(quick pause)

“Most people are surprised by how affordable it is. If the price makes sense for you, great. If not, there’s no obligation. Does it make sense to at least see what you qualify for?”

“I hear you. Most people who reach out are really just trying to make sure their family isn’t left with funeral or final expense costs. Is that what you were hoping to take care of?”

# Closing Objections

Objective: Confidently make recommendations and get a clear “yes” or “no”.

The worst outcome is a stall. Use the Scale of 1-5 Close for stall objections if necessary.

## “It’s Too Expensive” / “I Can’t Afford It”

“I understand. Let me clarify though, if it was more affordable, is this something you even want to have?”

(pause and wait for a positive response)

“Okay. So this isn’t about wanting it - it’s about making it work. Got it.”

“Well, I hope it’s clear by now, [Client Name], my goal is to make sure we protect your family in a way that fits your budget.”

“The most important thing is leaving them something.”

“You want them to know that even when things were tight, you did what you always did - you took care of them.”

“Here’s what I recommend. Let’s start with something affordable. Getting them \$5,000 is only [\$xx.xx] per month.”

“We’ll get you set up, get the policy mailed out, and make sure your family is covered.”

“If the choice is leaving them nothing, or leaving them \$5,000 that you intentionally set aside for them, which feels more like the way you want to be remembered?”

## “I still can’t afford that”

“I hear you. Would you agree that getting something in place is better than nothing?”

(let them answer)

“Ok. What is a monthly amount that you would feel comfortable with?”

## Stall: “I Need to Speak With My Spouse / Kids”

“Of course. I want you and [Spouse’s First Name] to feel completely comfortable. Is there any other information you’d need to feel comfortable moving forward?”

(wait for answer)

“Can I make a recommendation?”

(pause)

“From my experience, when people don’t get coverage in place today, they often procrastinate - and it never gets done.”

“On your family’s worst day, when they need someone to help them through this, it’s going to be me. They’ll have my number.”

“If you feel comfortable, I recommend we take care of this now. You can always make changes later - I’m just a phone call away.”

(Wait for their response. If still hesitant → Scale of 1–5 Close.)

## Stall: “I Need to Think About It”

“I understand, [First Name]. Is there anything about the coverage that’s unclear?”

“Is there anything you’re concerned about?”

(Address concerns if any.)

“My concern with waiting is that life gets busy, and coverage never gets put in place to protect [Beneficiary Name].”

(Pause → Scale of 1–5 Close if needed.)

## Stall: “I Want to Shop Around”

““I understand, [First Name]. Is there anything about the coverage that you think might be better with another company?”

(Pause and wait for a response)

Totally fair. My job is to make sure you don’t overpay. I’ve already compared every major carrier in your state, and I’m confident this is the best fit for your age and health.”

“Remember - your family gets me. On their worst day, I’ll be the one they call.”

“And I’ll level with you, when people don’t get coverage in place today, they often procrastinate to the extent that it never gets done. My concern is coverage never gets in place to protect [Beneficiary Name]”

“I recommend we get this in place. If you find something better, and I’m confident you won’t, you can always cancel. I’m just a phone call away.”

(Pause and let them respond. If still hesitant → Scale of 1–5 Close.)

## Scale of 1–5 Close (For “Stalls” Only)

## Purpose: Gauge commitment and uncover the real objection

“On a scale of 1 to 5 - where 1 means you don’t want this coverage at all and 5 means you’re absolutely getting it - where would you say you are?”

## Responses

● 1–2: “What would need to change to make this a 5?”

● 3: “What would need to change to make this a 5?”

● 4: “You’re close. What would need to change to make this a 5?”

● 5: “Great. Let’s get this off your plate. You have my number if you need anything.”

Respond to the real objection.

If they still stall, set firm follow ups with 4s and 5s. Nurture 1-3s.

# Application Objections

## “I Don’t Give Out My Banking Information”

“I completely understand - and you should be cautious.”

“Is it the security you’re concerned about - or just wanting to make sure nothing unexpected happens?”

(pause and wait for response)

## If Security

“Ok. I want you to feel good about this. Every insurance company we work with uses the same secure billing system as Social Security and pensions.”

“Your banking information goes directly into their encrypted system to activate coverage. We don’t store it.”

## If Something Unexpected

“Ok. I want you to feel good about this.”

“The only thing that ever comes out is the exact monthly amount we agree on - nothing more, nothing sooner.”

“There are no surprise fees, no changes without notice, and no one can touch the account without your permission.”

“If anything ever looks off, you’re not locked in - you can stop or change it with one phone call.”

## If they still won’t provide it:

“Okay, well we really can’t go much further if we don’t have the banking information to draft the first payment. Can I ask, what are you concerned about?”

## “I Don’t Have My Banking Information”

## Ask in order:

“Do you have your checkbook?”

“Do you receive bank statements by mail?”

“Did the bank give you a card with routing and account numbers?”

“Do you have online banking access?”

“I can also call the bank and mute myself while you ask.”

## “I Don’t Give Out My Social Security Number”

“I completely understand - and you should be cautious.”

“Let me explain briefly. Life insurance is regulated by the state of [Client State]- and every life insurance company is required to collect a social security number to verify your identity and make sure benefits are paid to the right people. ”

“Every family in America that’s protected their family with life insurance has had to attach their social security number to their application”

“Your information goes directly into the insurance company’s encrypted system and is kept secure.”

“Does that make sense?” (quick pause) Are you comfortable moving forward so we can get this issued correctly?”

(Pause and let them respond)

## If still hesitant:

“What is your main concern?”

(Pause and let them respond. Address any concerns.)

“How do you feel we can resolve this together, so that you can put the protection in place for your family? Because we can’t move forward without it.”

(Pause and let them respond)

“Would you like me to call the insurance company so they can confirm it’s required?”
