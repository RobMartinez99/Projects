## ATLAS ROUTING GUIDANCE — ETHOS

### When to Route to ETHOS

Route to ETHOS when:

1. The client cannot qualify for Americo, MoO, or Corebridge due to health history — meaning all three simplified issue options are off the table.
2. The client is age 45–80 and the face amount needed is $20K or less. This is the TruStage GAWL lane — guaranteed issue, no health questions.
3. The client is age 18–75, in average to fair health, and the face amount is in the $5K–$100K range (under 70) or $5K–$50K (70–75). This is the TruStage Advantage WL lane — simplified issue with instant decision.
4. The client has a condition that knocked them out of the carrier priority stack (Americo Eagle Guaranteed was still declined, or MoO Living Promise was declined) and they still need coverage.

ETHOS is Carrier #4 in the stack. Do not skip to ETHOS while Americo, MoO, or Corebridge still have a viable route for the client.

---

### When Not to Route to ETHOS

Do NOT route to ETHOS when:

- The client needs GI coverage above $20K. TruStage GAWL caps at $20K. There is no current GI solution in the MC stack above that amount.
- The client is a Florida resident seeking an IUL — Ethos IUL is NOT available in FL.
- The client is in good health and qualifies for Americo Eagle Premier or MoO Living Promise. ETHOS is not a step-up product for healthy clients.
- The client needs a specific term product — term is outside MC's core final expense and permanent coverage scope.
- The client is under age 45 and cannot qualify for any simplified issue product. TruStage GAWL does not cover under-45 clients. In this case, the stack has a gap — flag for Rob.

---

### What to Confirm Before Routing to ETHOS

Before presenting ETHOS to a client, confirm:

- [ ] Client's exact age (last birthday) — must be 45–80 for GAWL, 18–85 for Advantage WL
- [ ] Required face amount — must be $20K or under for GAWL, up to $100K (age <70) or $50K (age 70–75) for Advantage WL
- [ ] Client's state — confirm FL is acceptable (both TruStage products are available in FL)
- [ ] Client's health situation — if they failed simplified issue at Americo/MoO/Corebridge, document why, then confirm GAWL is appropriate as a guaranteed issue fallback
- [ ] Whether client accepts the graded benefit — for GAWL, the first 2 years pay back premiums + 10%, not the full face amount. Client must understand this before application.

---

### What Atlas Must Never Imply

- Do not imply ETHOS offers GI coverage above $20K. It does not.
- Do not present TruStage GAWL as equivalent to full Day 1 coverage — the 2-year graded benefit is a real limitation that must be disclosed.
- Do not route a Florida client to the Ethos IUL product under any circumstances — it is excluded from FL.
- Do not present ETHOS as the first option. It is Carrier #4. The client's first offer should always come from the priority stack (Americo → MoO → Corebridge).
- Do not guarantee approval on TruStage Advantage WL — it has underwriting questions and 3rd party evidence checks. GAWL is the only true guarantee.

**ETHOS Carrier Fit Rules — Atlas Reference**

**Use ETHOS when:**
- All three priority carriers (Americo, MoO, Corebridge) cannot cover the client
- Client is age 45–80 and needs GI coverage up to $20K (TruStage GAWL)
- Client is age 18–75, average to fair health, and needs simplified issue whole life up to $100K (TruStage Advantage WL)

- GI need exceeds $20K — no product in the ETHOS platform covers this
- Client is a FL resident and the product discussed is Ethos IUL — it is excluded from FL

**Disclosure requirement — GAWL graded benefit:**
Before any GAWL application, Atlas must confirm that Rob has disclosed the graded benefit to the client: years 1–2 return premiums plus 10%; full face amount begins in year 3. This is not optional — it is a material policy limitation.

**GI gap — above $20K:**
The current MC carrier stack has no GI solution above $20K. If a client needs guaranteed issue above that threshold, Atlas should flag this to Rob as an unresolved gap, not attempt to route the client to a non-existent option.
